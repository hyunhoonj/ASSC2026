                                             Welcome message from Chairs

                                             Dear Participants,
                                             It is our great pleasure to welcome you to the 29th Annual Meeting of
                                             the Association for the Scientific Study of Consciousness in Santiago,
                                             Chile. We are delighted to gather in a city of remarkable geographical,
                                             cultural, academic, and scientific significance. At the foot of the Andes,
                                             whose presence has long shaped the landscape and imagination
                                             of Chile, Santiago offers a unique setting in which to reflect on the
                                             nature of consciousness, bringing together perspectives from across
                                             disciplines, methods, and traditions.

                                             As one of Latin America’s major academic and cultural centers, Santiago
                                             is home to a vibrant ecosystem of universities, research institutes, and
      1                                      interdisciplinary communities contributing to psychology, neuroscience,
                                             philosophy, medicine, engineering, and the cognitive sciences. Its
                                             growing neuroscience community, strengthened by national and
                                             international collaborations, provides an inspiring backdrop for this
                                             year’s meeting and for the exchange of ideas at the heart of ASSC.




      29th Annual Meeting of the ASSC
                                             The program for ASSC29 reflects the richness and diversity of our field.
                                             We are especially pleased to feature keynote lectures by Kia Nobre,
                                             Tristan Bekinschtein, Felipe de Brigard, Kim Kuypers, James Mac Shine,
                                             and Lucia Melloni, spanning an exciting range of topics from cognitive
                                             neuroscience and consciousness to computational approaches,
                                             philosophical perspectives, and clinical and translational questions.

                                             Presidential Talk
                                             Kia Nobre, Department of Psychology, Yale University, USA

                                             Kia Nobre is a cognitive neuroscientist at Yale University, where she directs the
                                             Center for Neurocognition and Behavior at the Wu Tsai Institute. Her current
                                             research examines how the brain dynamically and proactively prioritizes and selects
                                             information from the sensory stream and from memories at various time scales to
                                             form psychological experience and guide behavior. Kia grew up in Rio de Janeiro.
                                             She obtained her PhD at Yale (1993, supervised by Greg McCarthy) and completed
                                             postdoctoral research at Harvard Medical School (with Marsel Mesulam). Before
                                             joining Yale (2023), Kia spent many (~30) years at the University of Oxford (1994-
                                             2023), latterly as Chair of Translational Cognitive Neuroscience and director of the
                                             Oxford Centre for Human Brain Activity. Kia’s research and scientific leadership
                                             have been recognized by memberships to international learned societies (Academia
                                             Europaea, British Academy, National Academy of Sciences, American Academy of
      4                                      Arts & Sciences) and various prestigious prizes, including the Broadbent Prize from
                                             the European Society for Cognitive Psychology (2019), C.L. de Carvalho-Heineken
                                             Award for Cognitive Science (2022), Fred Kavli Distinguished Career Contributions
                                             Award from the Cognitive Neuroscience Society (2024). Her mentoring abilities and
                                             dedication have also been recognized by a Lifetime Mentor Award (2022) from the
                                             Association for Psychological Science (APS).

                                             Keynote Speakers
                                             Tristan Bekinschtein, Department of Psychology, University of Cambridge, UK




June 30 to July 3, 2026, Santiago de Chile
                                             Tristan Bekinschtein is an Anglo-Argentinean biologist, Master in Neurophysiology
                                             and PhD in neuroscience, Buenos Aires University. He is a university professor and
                                             Turing Fellow at Cambridge University. Bekinschtein is primarily known for his work
                                             on variable states of consciousness and auditory feedback. He presently runs the
                                             Consciousness and Cognition Laboratory at Cambridge University.


                                             Felipe De Brigard, Duke Institute for Brain Sciences, Duke University, UK

                                             Felipe De Brigard, PhD is Professor in the departments of Philosophy and Psychology
                                             and Neuroscience and in the Center for Cognitive Neuroscience at Duke University.
                                             He is also Principal Investigator of the Imagination and Modal Cognition Laboratory
                                             (IMC Lab) within the Duke Institute for Brain Sciences. He has also received a
                                             number of awards, including being named Rising Star by the American Psychological
                                             Association, the Stanton Prize by the Society for Philosophy and Psychology, and
                                             the Early Career award by the Psychonomic Society. His research focuses on the
                                             nature of memory and its relations to other cognitive faculties, such as perception,
                                             imagination, attention and consciousness, and he is also interested in the
                                             foundations of neuroscience and moral psychology.
                                             Kim Kuypers, Faculty of Psychology and Neuroscience Maastricht University,
                                             The Netherlands

                                             Kim PC Kuypers is an Associate Professor at Maastricht University and Head of the
                                             Department of Neuropsychology and Psychopharmacology. Her research focuses
                                             on the effects of psychedelics and breathwork, employing experimental controlled
                                             designs and survey-based methods to study their use and impact. She aims to
                                             advance personalized approaches to psychedelic-assisted interventions and mental
                                             well-being.


                                             James Mac Shine, Brain and Mind Center, The University of Sydney, Australia

                                             Mac Shine is a Professor of Systems Neuroscience in the Discipline of Neuroscience
                                             at The University of Sydney. His group focusses on mapping mechanistic
                                             neurobiological neural models to dynamical network signatures estimated from
                                             functional neuroimaging data. He completed his Ph.D. in cognitive neuroscience
                                             at The University of Sydney, Australia. As a graduate student, Shine helped to
      5                                      refine the neural mechanisms of non-motor symptoms of Parkinson’s disease.
                                             As a postdoctoral fellow at Stanford University, Shine developed innovative
                                             approaches for tracking whole-brain network dynamics from non-invasive functional
                                             neuroimaging data. In 2017, Shine returned to The University of Sydney, where he
                                             now runs a diverse research lab that creates neurobiological models of cognitive
                                             function. He is a joint-NHMRC/Bellberry fellow.




      29th Annual Meeting of the ASSC
                                             Lucia Melloni, Ruhr University Bochum

                                             Dr. Lucia Melloni received her PhD in Psychology from the Universidad Católica in
                                             Santiago, having conducted part of her doctoral research at the Max Planck Institute
                                             for Brain Research in Frankfurt, Germany. She is a Principal Investigator and head of




June 30 to July 3, 2026, Santiago de Chile
                                             the Predictive Brain Department at Ruhr-Universität Bochum, with appointments at
                                             NYU Grossman School of Medicine and the CIFAR Brain, Mind & Consciousness
                                             Program. Before joining Bochum, she led a research group at the Max Planck
                                             Institute for Empirical Aesthetics in Frankfurt. Her lab is broadly interested in
                                             understanding the neural underpinnings of how we see (perception), how and why
                                             we experience what we see (consciousness), and how those experiences become
                                             imprinted in our brain (learning and memory), as well as the interplay between these
                                             processes. To this end, her lab combines intracranial (ECoG, laminar), neuroimaging,
                                             and, most recently, mobile, real-world recording methods. She is a leading architect
                                             of adversarial collaboration in consciousness science, co-founding the Cogitate
                                             Consortium to test competing theories under open, preregistered conditions. She is
                                             delighted to return to Santiago, where her scientific journey began.
